## 1. Template Structure

Every post follows this skeleton:

```
[HOOK: 1 sentence, max 2 lines visible before "see more"]

[BLOCK 1: Setup — 1-3 sentences]

[BLOCK 2: Development — 1-3 sentences]

[BLOCK 3: Insight/Turn — 1-3 sentences]

[BLOCK 4 (optional): Closing thought — 1-2 sentences]

[SIGNATURE or QUESTION or MIC-DROP]

#tag1 #tag2 #tag3
```

Max 3 sentences per block. White space between every block. Mobile-first readability.

## 2. Monday — Behind the Scenes

**Objective**: Trust, transparency, differentiation
**Register**: Curious, behind-the-curtains, sometimes vulnerable
**Length**: 600-1000 chars (standard)

### Hook patterns
- "This week the system did something I didn't predict."
- "[Day/time]. [Something broke/surprised/changed]."
- "Here's what 21 automations actually look like on a Monday morning."

### Body structure
- What happened (specific event, not abstract)
- What you learned or what surprised you
- What you changed as a result

### Closing
- Signature phrase OR reflective question
- First comment: link to detailed blog post if available

### Example skeleton
```
Monday. The engagement skill processed 43 notifications in 12 seconds.

The problem: 38 of those were "nice post" reactions.
The signal was drowning in noise.

I added a filter layer. 3 lines.
Now it only flags comments with questions or disagreements.

(The irony? The best interactions come from people who push back.)

[SIGNATURE]

#Automation #AI #LinkedIn
```

## 3. Tuesday — Tool/Workflow

**Objective**: Practical utility, saves, shares
**Register**: Pragmatic, generous, collegial
**Length**: 800-1200 chars (standard to long)

### Hook patterns
- "Here's the workflow that saves me [X] hours/week."
- "[Tool] + [Tool] + [Tool]. That's the stack."
- "The setup takes 15 minutes. The ROI is permanent."

### Body structure
- The problem before the workflow
- The solution (specific tools, steps, numbers)
- The result (measurable: time, money, quality)

### Closing
- "The system works. You make it start." OR specific question about reader's workflow
- First comment: complementary tip or question

## 4. Wednesday — Hot Take

**Objective**: Engagement, comments, visibility
**Register**: Indignant, provocative, morally clear
**Length**: 600-900 chars (standard, punchy)

### Hook patterns
- "I saw [thing]. It's [judgment]. Here's why."
- "Unpopular opinion: [statement that challenges consensus]."
- "Everyone's talking about [X]. Nobody's asking [Y]."

### Body structure
- The observation (what you saw that triggered this)
- The position (clear, unambiguous, not "on the other hand")
- The evidence (1-2 data points or examples)
- The implication (what happens if this continues)

### Closing
- Mic-drop or provocative question
- First comment: additional data point or "What's your experience with this?"

**Critical rule**: Wednesday MUST include a moral/ethical stance. Not just analysis — a position. "This is wrong because X" or "This worries me."

## 5. Thursday — Case Study

**Objective**: Social proof, credibility, specific results
**Register**: Proud but specific, detail-oriented
**Length**: 900-1300 chars (long, the data justifies it)

### Hook patterns
- "Last Thursday a [client type] told me: '[specific quote]'."
- "[Number] hours. [Number] automations. [Number] weeks. Here's the breakdown."
- "Before: [pain]. After: [result]. The delta: [number]."

### Body structure
- The situation (who, what problem, context)
- The approach (what you built, specific tools)
- The result (numbers: hours saved, cost reduced, revenue gained)
- The insight (what this teaches about the broader pattern)

### Specific anecdote rule
Never write "a client saved 40 hours." Write "Thursday, a consultant wrote me: 'For the first time in 6 months I had lunch without the laptop.'" The specific detail (day, object, sensation) creates credibility.

### Closing
- Data-driven closing + signature
- First comment: complementary insight + soft CTA to consultation

## 6. Friday — How-To

**Objective**: Maximum value, saves, reach
**Register**: Didactic, patient, thorough
**Length**: 1000-1300 chars (longest of the week)

### Hook patterns
- "How to [achieve result] in [timeframe]. Step by step."
- "The [tool/method] setup nobody explains clearly."
- "You're using [tool] wrong. Here's the right way."

### Body structure
- The end result (what the reader will achieve)
- Step-by-step (3-5 steps, each 1-2 sentences)
- The trick most people miss (the non-obvious insight)
- Where to go deeper (blog post, documentation)

### Closing
- Question: "What's your setup?" or "Which step are you stuck on?"
- First comment: link to full blog post/tutorial

## 7. Saturday — Storytelling

**Objective**: Human connection, followers, trust
**Register**: Personal, reflective, tender
**Length**: 600-1000 chars (standard)

### Hook patterns
- "[Time reference]. [Personal scene]."
- "I keep thinking about something that happened [when]."
- "Have you ever automated something and then missed it?"

### Body structure
- The scene (a specific moment, with sensory details)
- The realization (what it taught you)
- The broader lesson (applicable to the reader)

### Closing
- Reflective question or bare imperative ("Listen more. Write less.")
- First comment: personal follow-up thought

## 8. Sunday — Soft CTA

**Objective**: Conversion, product awareness
**Register**: Direct, confident, no-pressure
**Length**: 500-800 chars (short to standard)

### Hook patterns
- "The [resource/tool] I wish existed when I started."
- "But the Skills you talk about... where do I learn to build them?"
- "[Question from a real DM]. Here's my answer."

### Body structure
- The problem/question (something your audience recognizes)
- Your solution (the product, briefly and with specifics)
- The proof (1 data point or testimonial)

### CTA rule
The CTA goes in the FIRST COMMENT, never in the post body. The post must have standalone value even without the CTA.

### Closing
- Signature or confident imperative
- First comment: "[Product name]: [link] — [1-line description with a number]"

## 9. Micro-Post Template

**Frequency**: At least 1 per week
**Length**: 200-400 chars (3-6 bare sentences)
**Structure**: One thought. No framework. No bullets.

### Examples
```
I spent 3 hours debugging a skill that should have worked first try.
It didn't.
The problem wasn't the code.
It was the assumption I'd baked into it.
```

```
The best comment I got this week wasn't "great post."
It was "I disagree because [specific reason]."
That's the signal.
```

```
21 automations.
Zero existed 8 months ago.
Every one of them replaced a task I used to do by hand.
The time came back. The quality went up.
And I didn't hire anyone.
```

## 10. First Comment Templates

### Value days (Tue, Wed, Fri, Sat)
```
[Follow-up question OR additional data point OR insight not in the post]
```
Never: "If you want to learn more..." or any CTA.

### Case study (Thu)
```
[Additional insight from the case]
If you want to see if this applies to your case: [link to consultation]
```

### CTA (Sun)
```
Full guide: [link] — [pages], [modules], [case studies with measured results].
```

### Behind the scenes (Mon) — when blog post exists
```
Full breakdown with all the data: [blog link]
```
