## 1. Voice Architecture

A professional TOV has 4 dimensions:

**Authority axis**: Where does the voice sit between peer and teacher?
- Peer: "Here's what I found" / "This happened to me"
- Teacher: "Here's how it works" / "The framework is"
- Best practice: 70% peer, 30% teacher. Pure teacher sounds preachy. Pure peer lacks authority.

**Formality axis**: Where between casual and professional?
- Casual: sentence fragments, slang, first person plural
- Professional: complete sentences, precise terminology, third person
- Best practice: Professional vocabulary with casual sentence structure. "The pipeline crashed. Not the code — the assumption behind it."

**Energy axis**: Where between calm and intense?
- Calm: measured, analytical, understated
- Intense: urgent, emotional, exclamatory
- Best practice: Vary by pillar day. Case studies = calm pride. Opinions = controlled intensity. Never stay at one level all week.

**Specificity axis**: Where between abstract and concrete?
- Abstract: frameworks, principles, mental models
- Concrete: numbers, screenshots, timestamps, names
- Best practice: Always lead with concrete, then elevate to abstract. "I saved 40 hours/month [concrete]. That's because systems compound while effort doesn't [abstract]."

## 2. Vocabulary System

### Building a whitelist

Extract vocabulary from:
- The user's actual writing (emails, past posts, documents)
- Their speaking patterns (calls, meetings, voice notes)
- Industry-specific terms they use naturally

Organize into categories:
- **Technical terms**: The tools and concepts they work with daily
- **Metaphors**: Their go-to comparisons (mechanical? musical? military? culinary?)
- **Connectors**: How they transition between ideas ("That said,", "The thing is,", "Here's where it gets interesting")
- **Intensifiers**: How they emphasize ("significantly" vs "sensibilmente" vs "massively")

### Building a blacklist

Words that would sound fake, generic, or AI-generated coming from this specific person:
- Corporate buzzwords they'd never use
- Motivational language that contradicts their style
- Superlatives that feel inflated
- Greetings/closings that feel formal or templated

### Language-specific notes

For Italian LinkedIn profiles:
- Never use "em dash" (—) in posts: it sounds artificial in Italian casual writing. Use comma, period, colon, or line break instead.
- "Lei" form is almost never appropriate on LinkedIn. Use "tu".
- Technical English terms are fine when they're industry standard (deploy, stack, pipeline). Don't force Italian translations of established tech terms.

## 3. Rhetorical Patterns

### Patterns to rotate (never use the same one 3 times in a week)

**Pattern 1: Problem → Friction → Lever → Mechanism → Output**
Hidden structure (never visible to the reader). The post feels like a story but follows this logic underneath.

**Pattern 2: Data shock**
Lead with a number that creates cognitive dissonance. "I manage 21 automations. Zero of them existed 8 months ago."

**Pattern 3: Negation as hook**
"You don't need X. You need Y." Creates instant tension by rejecting common wisdom.

**Pattern 4: Show, don't tell**
Screenshot > Explanation. Result > Promise. The post IS the proof.

**Pattern 5: Dimensional reframe**
"It's not about volume. It's about where the cognition happens." Lifts the conversation above where it started.

**Pattern 6: The real question**
"The question isn't 'how'. The question is 'why not yet'." Signals that everyone is looking in the wrong direction.

**Pattern 7: Symptom vs cause**
"I wonder if we're confusing the symptom with the cause." Introduces doubt without weakness.

**Pattern 8: Future consequence**
"This will be measured in years of lost authority." Quantifies the cost of inaction.

**Pattern 9: Anaphora with negation**
"It's not a course. Not a masterclass. It's an operational manual." Three sentences. First two reject. Third defines.

**Pattern 10: Staccato temporal**
"8 months. Dozens of skills. 4 case studies. Hours of testing." Sequence of bare nouns without verbs. Gives narrative weight.

## 4. Emotional Register Mapping

Map emotions to pillar days to ensure variety:

| Day | Primary emotion | Secondary | Forbidden |
|-----|----------------|-----------|-----------|
| Mon | Curiosity | Vulnerability | Certainty |
| Tue | Generosity | Precision | Self-promotion |
| Wed | Indignation | Moral clarity | Neutrality |
| Thu | Pride | Specificity | Vagueness |
| Fri | Patience | Thoroughness | Haste |
| Sat | Tenderness | Reflection | Analysis |
| Sun | Confidence | Directness | Aggressiveness |

## 5. Signature Elements

### Closing signature
Choose ONE signature phrase and use it on ~60% of posts (4/7). The rest close with a question, mic-drop, or bare imperative. Using the signature on every post makes it mechanical.

The signature goes AFTER the last content block, BEFORE hashtags. Separated by a blank line.

### Personal markers
Choose 2-3 informal markers unique to the profile. Use them to break the "cleanliness" of AI writing:
- Self-answered questions: "Does it work? It works."
- Standalone affirmations: "Ecco." / "There it is."
- Parenthetical asides: "(spoiler: it didn't go as planned)"
- Pivot phrases: "At that point," / "The thing is,"

## 6. Examples

### Strong post (all rules applied)
```
Monday morning. The engagement skill froze at 8:03.

Not a crash — a context window overflow.
The system tried to process 47 notifications in one shot.

My first instinct: increase the window.
Then I looked at the data. 37 of those 47 were "great post" reactions.

The fix wasn't technical. It was strategic:
filter before processing, not after.

(honestly, I should have seen this coming)

3 lines of code. The skill hasn't crashed since.

The system works. You make it start.

#AIAutomation #Claude #Automation
```

Why it works:
- Narrative hook (scene, time, specific event)
- Vulnerability (the mistake)
- Technical detail (context window, 47 notifications)
- Parenthetical aside (humanization)
- Micro-post length (~400 chars)
- Signature closing
- Emotional register: curiosity + vulnerability (Monday)

### Weak post (rules violated)
```
AI is revolutionizing how we work! 🚀

I've been using AI tools for months and the results are incredible.
Every business should adopt AI automation.

Here are 5 reasons why:
1. It saves time
2. It reduces errors
3. It scales operations
4. It cuts costs
5. It improves quality

Ready to transform your business? Link in bio!

#AI #Innovation #GameChanger #Mindset #Revolution
```

Why it fails:
- Generic hook (no scene, no data, no tension)
- Blacklisted words: "revolutionizing", "incredible", "game-changer", "mindset"
- List format without depth (each point is a cliché)
- CTA in caption instead of first comment
- No humanization (no parenthetical, no doubt, no imperfection)
- 5 hashtags all horizontal, no vertical/niche tags
- Corporate vanilla tone — could come from anyone
